Space begins 100 kilometres above the Earth's surface. The biggest challenge of spaceflight is reaching orbit, but what is an orbit?

When you throw a ball up in the air, gravity pulls it back down again. Launch a rocket through the air and into space, and if it is going slower than seven kilometres per second, it will fall down to Earth too.

However, if you get a rocket into space and travelling horizontally at almost eight kilometres per second, something cool happens! Switch off the engines, and the rocket coasts through space. Gravity bends the rocket's path so it goes around the Earth, but can't bend it enough to make the rocket crash into Earth. The rocket is in orbit!

Of course, getting anything travelling at almost eight kilometres per second is a huge challenge. That's 30 times faster than an airliner! Going too fast will break your orbit and send you off into space.
