Re-Entry

Perhaps the most dangerous part of any spaceflight is re-entry. To get home, you need to slow down from orbital speed to something more sedate.

How do you do this? The solution is to use the Earth's atmosphere to slow the spacecraft down. But there's a catch! Travelling through the air so fast can heat the spacecraft to more than 1000 degrees. Spacecraft need shielding against this temperature because it can weaken or melt most metals.

Once the spacecraft has slowed down to 'only' hundreds of kilometres per hour, landing is the next trick. Some spacecraft land using giant parachutes while others land like gliders.

Back on the ground, it's time to get used to weight again. Don't stand up too quickly! You don't want to end your trip by falling flat on your face!
