Imagining Space Flight

Have you imagined travelling into space? You wouldn't be the first, not by a long shot!

In the mythology of Ancient Greece, Icarus flies into the sky on wings made of feathers and wax. However, the wax melts when he gets too close to the Sun, and he falls back to Earth. Could you really fly too close to the Sun using wings? No. Wings need air to work, and planes struggle to fly more than 20 kilometres above the Earth.

In 1865, French author Jules Verne imagined launching men to the Moon using a giant cannon! Lots of people read his book 'From the Earth to the Moon' and it inspired many pioneers of spaceflight. However, a cannon launching a capsule into space would subject its occupants to lethal accelerations! Watch the clip of 'A Trip to the Moon' and see whether you can spot any other aspects of the journey which would be impossible in reality.
