Not all shooting stars are completely vaporised on their journey through the atmosphere. Some meteoroids are big enough to survive and reach the surface of the Earth, where they become meteorites. How do we find them? Good question! Some people are lucky enough to see a falling meteor or hear its sonic boom and then find a meteorite on the ground. The sonic boom is the noise it makes when it travels through our atmosphere at speeds faster than the speed of sound. Sometimes, it's still hot from almost burning up in the Earth's atmosphere.

Scientists also go hunting for meteorites that fell to Earth long ago. Where are the best places to find meteorites? Meteorites are easier to find in places without many rocks, trees and rivers, so if you thought of deserts and Antarctica, you're right! In Antarctica, the movement of glaciers can also concentrate meteorites to places, like the edges of mountain ranges that make them easier to find.

But finding meteorites is rarely easy, even after you've made the hazardous journey to some of the most extreme places on Earth!
