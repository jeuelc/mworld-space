What are the challenges of taking a spacewalk?

To truly explore space, people need to get out of their spacecraft. Walking in space isn't as easy as it sounds. A space suit has to protect you, provide air and survive the extreme temperatures of space. A tear in its fabric could be lethal!

Alexey Leonov took the first spacewalk from the Voskhod 2 capsule in March 1965. Leonov struggled to move in his spacesuit, which inflated like a balloon. He struggled mightily to get back inside the Voskhod 2 capsule, but after 12 minutes he did get back in and returned safely to Earth.

Ed White, the first American to walk in space, had a similar problem. He also moved around in space using a gas gun while fellow astronaut James McDivitt took photographs. White was having so much fun he needed some convincing to get back into the Gemini 4 spacecraft!
