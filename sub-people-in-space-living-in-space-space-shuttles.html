Space Shuttles

To make space travel a routine thing, you need an easy way to get there.

In the 1970s, the United States developed the space shuttle so they could get large numbers of people and cargo into space. The space shuttles were the size of airliners, flew into space on rockets, then returned to Earth like gliders.

The first space shuttle launch was in 1981, and over the next three decades there were 135 shuttle flights carrying hundreds of people into space! Space shuttles helped build the International Space Station and repair the Hubble Space Telescope.

However, the space shuttles didn't succeed in making space flight routine. It cost way more than people originally thought it would: each flight cost roughly half a billion dollars! The Challenger and Columbia accidents in 1986 and 2003 killed seven astronauts. Perhaps the space shuttles had more in common with experimental aircraft than flying trucks.
