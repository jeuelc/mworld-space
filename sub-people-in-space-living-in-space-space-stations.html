Unlike a cramped spacecraft, space stations are designed to be semi-comfortable places to live and work for months at a time.

The first space stations were sent into orbit by economic and scientific cooperation between the Soviet Union and United States in the early 1970s. Astronauts studied the effects of weightlessness on the human body. They also observed the Sun and stars, looking at types of light that are normally blocked by the Earth's air.

The largest space station ever built is the International Space Station. More than 15 countries are involved in that project. It took over 10 years to build in orbit and weighs as much as a loaded 747 plane. It usually has a crew of six people. You can see it from Earth when it passes overhead. It looks like a very bright star that crosses the sky in just a few minutes. Through cooperation the countries exploring space have achieved far more than they could separately.
