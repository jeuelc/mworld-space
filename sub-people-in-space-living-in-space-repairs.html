One of the most important parts of living in space is repairing your home. It isn't good if your home on Earth has a hole in the roof or lacks insulation. In space the consequences of a hole in the roof are even worse.

When the Skylab space station was launched, one solar panel was torn off, another jammed and much of the insulation was torn away. The first astronauts to visit Skylab arrived at a space station that was boiling inside and had little electrical power. To fix it, astronauts fitted Skylab with a special sun shade called a 'parasol' and unjammed the remaining solar panel during a spacewalk.

Since the 1970s, astronauts have built and repaired space stations. Fuel leaks and power failures have been fixed, prolonging the life of the space stations. More mundane repairs have been needed too, including fixing toilets on the International Space Station!
