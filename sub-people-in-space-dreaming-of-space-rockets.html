Rockets

Space is very empty. There is no air for wings or propellers to work in. Jet engines can't work in space because they burn their fuel in air.

Rockets solve the space travel problem. A rocket burns a combination of chemicals, such as hydrogen and oxygen, and these can burn even when the rocket is in space.

Rockets push themselves forward by blasting out a jet of hot gas, produced by burning an explosive combination of chemicals. A rocket can produce this blast of hot gas both on Earth and in space.
