In the late 1950s and early 1960s, it was a race to get the first person into space. Dozens of rockets were sent on test flights with empty capsules or animals on board. Some of the tests ended in disaster.

On 12 April 1961, Yuri Gagarin, a cosmonaut from the Soviet Union, was launched into space on board Vostok 1. He flew a single orbit of the Earth and safely returned to Earth less than two hours after his launch. Within 24 hours Gagarin's name was known around the world!

Less than one month later, Alan Shepard blasted off on board a Mercury capsule. The rocket carrying the Mercury capsule wasn't powerful enough to get into orbit though, so Shepard's flight lasted just 15 minutes.

By the end of 1963, more than a dozen people had travelled into space, including Valentina Tereshkova, the first woman in space.
