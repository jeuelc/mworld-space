Dangers and Disasters

Spaceflight is dangerous. Riding a flaming rocket containing hundreds of tonnes of explosive fuels isn't for the fainthearted! Spacecraft are some of the most advanced and complicated machines people make, and a single fault can potentially be fatal!

In 1967 two disasters struck. The crew of America's Apollo 1, including Ed White, were killed in a fire as they tested their spacecraft before launch. A few months later, Russian cosmonaut Vladimir Komarov died when the parachutes of his Soyuz 1 spacecraft failed to open.

Other disasters followed. Several astronauts were killed during training accidents and plane crashes, including Yuri Gagarin, the first man in space. The Soyuz 11, and the Challenger and Columbia space shuttle disasters killed another 17 astronauts.

Other disasters were averted by the narrowest of margins. Apollo 13 limped back to Earth after being battered by an explosion on the way to the Moon. The Soyuz T-10-1 spacecraft's escape system saved its crew just seconds before a catastrophic explosion!
